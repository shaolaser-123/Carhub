One eventually likes a car

Some cars don’t need to be fast, loud, or expensive to mean something to you. It usually starts quietly—maybe the way an engine sounds on a cold start, the way the steering feels just a bit more honest than you expected, or how the car seems to fit you without trying. Over time, it stops being “just a car” and starts becoming something familiar. Something you notice in parking lots without meaning to. Something you start preferring over others for no logical reason at all.
That’s how it happens. Not in one dramatic moment, but in small details you don’t forget. The way it pulls, the way it feels through corners, the way it makes even a normal drive feel a bit better than it should. And before you know it, you’re not just interested in it—you’re attached to it. That’s what this space is for. Your saved cars aren’t just a list. They’re the ones that stood out enough to stay in your head.
